Scope & Purpose ISO 9001:2015 ▾
Purpose

To define the correct procedure for cleaning the extruder die head and screw on CMP blow moulding machines at Yash Poly Plast. Proper cleaning prevents contamination of future batches, protects precision machine components from damage, and ensures machine longevity. This instruction applies whenever a material change, colour change, extended shutdown, or maintenance schedule requires head or screw cleaning.

Applies To

All CMP blow moulding machines at the Mahape facility. Applies during scheduled preventive maintenance, material or colour changeovers, and after any unplanned machine stoppage exceeding normal shift downtime.

Responsible Parties

Production Operator (assists, operates extruder screw during cleaning), Maintenance Technician (heads disassembly/reassembly), Production Supervisor (authorises cleaning, ensures safety precautions are in place)

2
Safety & PPE Requirements MANDATORY ▾

MANDATORY SAFETY REQUIREMENT: CRITICAL: All cleaning operations involve hot surfaces (barrel zone temperatures 100–220°C). Full PPE must be worn at all times. Only copper or brass rods must be used as cleaning tools — NEVER hard metal tools or emery cloth, which will permanently damage precision head and barrel surfaces. Use fume extractor hood or ensure adequate ventilation when plastic is in molten state. Disconnect power to heating elements before disassembly; reconnect only as described in procedure.

Heat-Resistant Gloves
Face Shield
Full-Arm Protective Clothing
Fume / Dust Mask

Process Flowchart — FLW-WI-PRD-05 FLW-WI-PRD-05 ▾
1
⚡ Critical Control PointSECTION A — DIE HEAD REMOVAL. Ensure adequate ventilation or fume extractor is running. Don full PPE.
Safety First
2
⚡ Critical Control PointSwitch off power to heating elements to disconnect electrical supply from all heater bands on the die head.
Isolate Power
3
Remove heater bands from the head. Disconnect all thermocouple connectors carefully — label if multiple zones.
Heater Removal
4
⚡ Critical Control PointFit eyebolt into the head connecting flange. Attach lifting cable and bring cable under tension using hoisting hook. Do not lift yet — cable is under tension only.
Rig Hoist
5
Slacken and remove the nuts/bolts holding the head flange to the extruder barrel flange. Work in diagonally opposite pairs.
Undo Flange Bolts
6
Carefully move die head away from the extruder using the hoist. Position head on the prepared work bench.
Remove Head
7
⚡ Critical Control PointConnect power supply near work bench to the head's heating elements to keep residual plastic in a molten state during cleaning.
Maintain Heat on Bench
8
SECTION B — DIE HEAD CLEANING. Switch on power to heating elements on the bench; allow head to heat up. Verify plastic is in molten state.
Heat Head
9
Disconnect power from heating elements. Disassemble heating elements from head. Disassemble head components per machine drawings (mandrel, nozzle, pin, body).
Disassemble Head
10
⚡ Critical Control PointUsing COPPER RODS ONLY (pointed, rounded, chisel shapes as required), remove all residual plastic from all head flow channels, surfaces, and mating faces. Do NOT use hard metal tools or emery cloth.
Clean with Cu Rods Only
11
Degrease and thoroughly clean all sliding components. Remove all traces of old grease and burnt plastic deposits.
Degrease Components
12
Lubricate threads of all screws with high-temperature grease before reassembly.
Grease Threads
13
⚡ Critical Control PointSECTION C — EXTRUDER SCREW CLEANING. Heat all barrel zones to 10–20°C higher than the normal processing temperature for the material being removed.
Raise Barrel Temp
14
Switch off power to heating elements. Confirm head has been moved away from the extruder (completed in Section A).
Head Away
15
Start extruder screw at 40 rpm. Pour cleaning/purge material into the hopper and allow it to run through.
Purge at 40 rpm
16
Reduce barrel temperature setpoints to 100°C. Reduce screw speed to 30 rpm. Continue running until cleaning material exits clear — without contaminated waste material.
Lower Temp & Speed
17
Position a suitable collection container at the extruder outfeed to catch expelled material.
Position Container
18
Operate extruder screw until hopper and barrel are completely empty. If residual material remains in hopper, blow out with compressed air.
Empty Barrel
19
Stop the extruder screw. Thread the special extractor tool onto the screw nose.
Fit Extractor
20
⚡ Critical Control PointIncrease barrel temperature setpoints to 10% above normal working value. Allow screw to heat. Using extractor tool, pull screw out of the barrel. Place screw on the work bench.
Extract Screw
21
SECTION D — BARREL CLEANING. Reduce barrel temperature to 100°C. Insert a suitable diameter brush into the barrel and brush out residual material. Follow with compressed air inside barrel.
Brush Barrel
22
⚡ Critical Control PointUse a beam lamp to inspect inside the barrel — the bore should appear bright and clean throughout its length. No dark residue or deposit should be visible.
Beam Lamp Inspection
23
Slide a clean cylindrical piece wrapped in clean lint-free rags through the barrel bore to complete final cleaning and wipe-down.
Final Wipe
24
⚡ Critical Control PointSECTION E — SCREW REINSTALLATION. Slide the cleaned screw back into the barrel spindle without forcing. Turn screw by hand to engage the longitudinal key into the groove in the reduction gear.
Reinstall Screw
25
⚡ Critical Control PointSECTION F — HEAD REINSTALLATION. Move the cleaned and reassembled die head back up to the extruder. Ensure head is perpendicular to the extruder barrel. Ensure flange thread does not protrude beyond the head thread.
Align Head
26
Apply high-temperature grease to the threads of all flange nuts. Move head flange up to the extruder barrel flange, ensuring flanges are parallel before engaging.
Grease & Align Flanges
27
⚡ Critical Control PointFit and hand-tighten all flange nuts. Then tighten fully in diagonally opposite pairs to ensure even clamping force and prevent leaks.
Tighten Diagonally
28
Reconnect heater bands and thermocouples. Gradually increase temperatures to production setpoints. Do not rush temperature ramp-up.
Heat to Production Temp
29
⚡ Critical Control PointOnce at temperature, check and re-tighten all head flange bolts in diagonally opposite pairs. Thermal expansion may require re-torqueing.
Final Torque Check
30
Run trial shots. Check parison quality and appearance. Record cleaning completion in the PM log.
Trial & Record

दायरा एवं उद्देश्य ISO 9001:2015 ▾
उद्देश्य

यश पॉली प्लास्ट में सीएमपी ब्लो मोल्डिंग मशीनों पर एक्सट्रूडर डाई हेड और स्क्रू की सफाई के लिए सही प्रक्रिया को परिभाषित करना। उचित सफाई भविष्य के बैचों के संदूषण को रोकती है, सटीक मशीन घटकों को क्षति से बचाती है, और मशीन की दीर्घायु सुनिश्चित करती है। यह निर्देश तब लागू होता है जब किसी सामग्री में परिवर्तन, रंग परिवर्तन, विस्तारित शटडाउन, या रखरखाव कार्यक्रम में सिर या पेंच की सफाई की आवश्यकता होती है।

||| पर लागू होता है महापे सुविधा में सभी सीएमपी ब्लो मोल्डिंग मशीनें। निर्धारित निवारक रखरखाव, सामग्री या रंग परिवर्तन के दौरान और सामान्य शिफ्ट डाउनटाइम से अधिक किसी भी अनियोजित मशीन स्टॉपेज के बाद लागू होता है।

जिम्मेदार पार्टियाँ

उत्पादन संचालक (सहायक, सफाई के दौरान एक्सट्रूडर स्क्रू को संचालित करता है), रखरखाव तकनीशियन (सिरों को अलग करना/पुनः जोड़ना), उत्पादन पर्यवेक्षक (सफाई को अधिकृत करता है, सुनिश्चित करता है कि सुरक्षा सावधानियां बरती जाएं)

सुरक्षा एवं पीपीई आवश्यकताएँ

2
अनिवार्य ▾ अनिवार्य सुरक्षा आवश्यकता:

गंभीर: सभी सफाई कार्यों में गर्म सतहें (बैरल क्षेत्र का तापमान 100-220 डिग्री सेल्सियस) शामिल होती हैं। हर समय पूर्ण पीपीई पहनना चाहिए। सफाई उपकरणों के रूप में केवल तांबे या पीतल की छड़ों का उपयोग किया जाना चाहिए - कभी भी कठोर धातु के उपकरण या उभरे हुए कपड़े का उपयोग नहीं करना चाहिए, जो सटीक सिर और बैरल सतहों को स्थायी रूप से नुकसान पहुंचाएगा। जब प्लास्टिक पिघली हुई अवस्था में हो तो फ्यूम एक्सट्रैक्टर हुड का उपयोग करें या पर्याप्त वेंटिलेशन सुनिश्चित करें। जुदा करने से पहले हीटिंग तत्वों की बिजली काट दें; प्रक्रिया में वर्णित अनुसार ही पुनः कनेक्ट करें। 02 ताप

Section 4: Root Cause Analysis — Grade-Specific
#CheckCriteriaMethodResultRemarks
4.1
Varroc 1 Ltr Black
Root cause verified through investigationPhysical inspection of production process and material1. Minor impurities in raw material — hopper contamination and improper regrind handling introduced foreign matter into the melt.
2. Parison short at bottle base — insufficient parison programming length at the bottom zone caused incomplete wall coverage, resulting in pinholes near the base seam.
4.2
Arzol 800ml Gold
Root cause verified through investigationPhysical inspection of mould and machine parameters1. Mould pinch-off inserts worn — incomplete sealing at the base seam leaving micro-gaps in the bottle wall.
2. Blow pressure set below required value for this grade — parison did not conform fully to the mould surface, leaving thin unwelded spots.
4.3
5 Ltr MFC
Root cause verified through investigationObservation of finishing process and inspection station1. New/unskilled workers using improper knife angle and uncontrolled cutting stroke during handle finishing, causing knife cuts that penetrate the bottle wall.
2. Inspection carried out under insufficient lighting — pinholes and cut marks not visually detectable at the inspection station.
4.4
1.5 Ltr Arzol Grey
Root cause verified through investigationMould and ejection cycle analysisDotted mould pattern gripping the warm bottle surface during ejection before adequate cooling. Material pulled away from dot locations creating micro-holes. Ejection initiated before the bottle wall had sufficiently solidified at the dot features.
4.5
Systemic / Organisational Root Causes (All Grades)
Common systemic factors identifiedCross-grade analysis1. Labour shortage — insufficient manpower per shift leading to rushed production and skipped quality checks.
2. Improper in-process quality checking — QC inspection not consistently followed on the production line.
3. Hopper not loaded leading to dry run — material exhaustion mid-production causing parison defects (short shots, voids, pinholes) across multiple grades.
4. Lack of skill in new workers — recently joined operators not yet proficient in finishing, inspection, or process parameter monitoring.		These systemic issues are the primary contributing factor across all four grade complaints.

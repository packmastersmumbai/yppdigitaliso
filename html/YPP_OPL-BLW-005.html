Key Points Covered 1 steps ▾

1
MINIMUM WALL THICKNESS STANDARD (IS 7926 / Customer Spec)
1L bottle = 1.2 mm minimum. 5L can = 2.0 mm minimum. 20L drum = 2.8 mm minimum. Measure using ultrasonic wall thickness gauge at 8 positions: Left / Right / Front / Back at mid-body AND at base. Record every inspection on form YPP_FRM-BLW-QC-01.
3
Defect Diagnosis & Corrective Actions 5 defect types ▾
Defect Type 1 — Overall Thin Walls (all 8 positions below minimum)
Severity: HIGH
Root CauseHow to IdentifyCorrective Action
Screw RPM too low — insufficient parison weightWeigh 5 consecutive parisons — average below target weightIncrease screw RPM by 5 RPM. Observe 10 cycles before further change.
Back pressure insufficient — poor melt compactionParison weight low and inconsistent (>2% variation)Increase back pressure by 5 kgf/cm². Observe 10 cycles.
Regrind exceeding 25% — lower bulk densityRegrind bag ratio in hopper blend >25%Reduce regrind to maximum 25%. Weigh regrind and virgin separately before blending.
Blow pressure too high — parison over-stretchedWalls thin at all positions; parison waist visible in mould open shotReduce blow pressure by 0.5 bar. Minimum: 6.5 bar for standard containers.
Worn mould pinch-off — excessive thinning at baseFlash at parting line is thin and tears rather than cuts cleanlyCheck pinch-off land width — minimum 1.5 mm for 5L. Send mould for re-machining if worn.
Defect Type 2 — Thick One Side / Thin Other Side (directional non-uniformity)
Severity: HIGH
Root CauseHow to IdentifyCorrective Action
Parison off-centre — die head misalignedWall thick on one side, thin on opposite side. Parison curves before entering mould.Correct parison alignment using die head Allen bolts A/B/C. Refer to OPL-003.
HMI parison profile asymmetric — unequal zone valuesDirectional variation in mid-body or shoulder despite straight parisonCheck parison profile on Beckhoff HMI. Points 1–5 = shoulder, 10–15 = body, 15–20 = base. Adjust thin-side zone by +3% to +5%. Observe 10 cycles.
Mould cooling uneven — one side cools fasterThin wall consistently on one side across many bottles; parison alignment is correctCheck cooling water flow rates on left vs right mould channels. Balance flow using needle valves.
Defect Type 3 — Thin at Base Only
Severity: MEDIUM
Root CauseHow to IdentifyCorrective Action
Insufficient material in base zone of parisonBase wall <minimum on all 4 base positions; mid-body OKIncrease HMI parison profile points 17–20 by +5%. Observe 10 cycles.
Insufficient cooling at baseBase wall thin but correct weight — cooling not solidifying material before mould opensCheck base plate cooling connection and flow. Increase mould close time by 0.3 sec.
Mould opening too early — base not fully setBottles deform at base after ejection; base feels soft immediately after ejectionIncrease mould close time by 0.3 sec increments until base is firm on ejection.
Blow pin entry angle incorrectThin patch at base off-centre; not uniform around circumferenceCheck blow pin vertical alignment. Blow pin must be centred within ±0.1 mm of mould axis.
Defect Type 4 — Pearlescent or Laminated Wall (layers separating)
Severity: CRITICAL — Reject Lot
Root CauseHow to IdentifyCorrective Action
Excessive regrind or mixed-grade regrind (incompatible MFI)Pearlescent sheen on surface; wall splits into layers under squeeze testReduce regrind to 0%. Run 5 kg natural HDPE purge through barrel. Check regrind for contamination before re-use.
Degraded material in barrel — long residence time at temperatureLamination plus brown streaks; MFI of output higher than input materialPurge barrel completely with 5 kg natural HDPE. Check all heater zone setpoints — no zone above 190°C. Check that machine was not left with full barrel stagnant for >20 minutes at process temperature.
Wall Thickness Measurement Procedure — 8 Positions
Every 30 min or 500 pieces
PositionLocation on BottleMinimum Spec (5L can)Action if Below Spec
1Mid-body — Left side2.0 mmSTOP — raise NCR
2Mid-body — Right side2.0 mmSTOP — raise NCR
3Mid-body — Front2.0 mmSTOP — raise NCR
4Mid-body — Back2.0 mmSTOP — raise NCR
5Base — Left side2.0 mmSTOP — raise NCR
6Base — Right side2.0 mmSTOP — raise NCR
7Base — Front2.0 mmSTOP — raise NCR
8Base — Back2.0 mmSTOP — raise NCR
🛑
STOP RULE: If measured wall thickness falls below minimum at ANY of the 8 positions — STOP production immediately. Quarantine all containers from last passed inspection to the defective piece. Tag as NC (Non-Conforming). Raise NCR on form YPP_FRM-NCR-01. Do not dispatch until wall thickness is confirmed within specification on corrected production.

Comprehension Check Q&A ▾
  1. You measure a 5L HDPE can and find 1.7 mm at base-right and 2.3 mm at base-left. What is the most likely root cause, and which OPL should you refer to for the first corrective action?
  2. A 1L bottle shows 1.0 mm wall at mid-body on all 4 positions. (a) Is this a nonconformance? (b) What immediate actions must be taken? (c) Which form must be raised?
  3. What is the maximum regrind percentage allowed in the hopper blend, and what defect type does exceeding this limit typically cause?
